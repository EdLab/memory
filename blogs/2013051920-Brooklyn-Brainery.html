Brooklyn Brainery

EdLab Studios

May 19, 2013


Entering the workforce as an adult can often mean that there is less and less time to learn new skills. A self-described "book club on steroids," the Brooklyn Brainery offers a solution for these time-constrained adults with "after-work" classes ranging from Japanese to knitting. The brainchild of a few friends who maybe just wanted to learn everything, the Brooklyn Brainery's model is all about accessibility, student initiative, collaboration, and instructor volunteers. Via the Brainery's website, potential students and teachers alike suggest what they might want to learn or teach, and the Brainery just helps organize the classes and provides the space. Classes last for one to four weeks: short, sweet, encapsulated lessons on anything to give students an idea if they want to pursue the topic further or learn enough to know they donâ€™t care to learn any more. Much like Skillshare but with a permanent home, a rotating cast, and a growing community of students, the Brooklyn Brainery provides a space for adults to pickup skills and knowledge within a loosely-structured environment.
