Minority Teens As Infomediaries

chingfu

July 23, 2014


According to a study on usage and services at U.S. libraries, about two-thirds of those who use computers there use them to help family members, friends or other people find information on education, healthcare, or career and learning opportunities. Findings also reveal that non-native English speaking minority youth are more likely to be information mediaries for others. Understanding these teensâ€™ experiences in using library technologies to help others is crucial to creating better information services for these overlooked patrons. However, little research has been done to explore this area.

InfoMe is a research project that aims to understand this phenomenon of youth-as-infomediaries (InfoMes). In addition to traditional research approaches such as surveys, InfoMe includes unique Teen Design Days (TDD), workshops that employ design thinking to help researchers learn "how and why immigrant and refugee youth act as InfoMes within their social networks, especially their ethnic communities, and how they can be supported through technology and information services." This video captures one of the Teen Design Day workshops in practice.
